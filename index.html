📉 What to Expect at Each Dose

▼

300mg → 150mg (First Reduction)

This is usually the biggest adjustment. Your brain has been used to a higher dopamine/norepinephrine boost.

  • Week 1–2: Possible fatigue, mild headaches, irritability, or feeling "flat." This is normal and temporary.
  • Week 2–4: Most people stabilize. Energy and mood start to level out at the new baseline.
  • Appetite: May increase slightly — Wellbutrin suppresses appetite, so reducing the dose can reverse this.
  • Sleep: May temporarily improve or feel different as stimulating effects decrease.

150mg → 0mg (Final Step)

Good news: Wellbutrin has far fewer discontinuation symptoms than SSRIs. No brain zaps, minimal physical symptoms.

  • Week 1–2: Low motivation, fatigue, possible mood dip. This is the drug leaving — not "you."
  • Week 2–4: Your brain starts producing its own dopamine regulation again. Most people notice this lifting.
  • Week 4–8: Authentic baseline emerges. Many people feel more emotionally connected and like "themselves" again.
  • Some taper 150mg → 75mg first if the 150→0 step feels too big (requires IR pills, discuss with prescriber).

✅ After Stopping Completely

  • 2–4 weeks: Physical adjustment mostly complete. Energy stabilizing.
  • 4–6 weeks: Mood settles to your natural baseline. Appetite normalizes fully.
  • 6–12 weeks: Full picture of how you feel without medication. Give it this long before evaluating.

⚠️ Symptoms to Know About

▼

Common (Expected)

  • Fatigue / low energy
  • Irritability or mood swings
  • Headaches (usually mild)
  • Increased appetite
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Vivid or unusual dreams
  • Temporary mood dip

Less Common

  • Mild anxiety uptick
  • Lightheadedness
  • Flu-like feeling
  • Muscle tension
  • Nausea (uncommon)
  • Lowered motivation temporarily

👍 What Wellbutrin Doesn't Usually Cause (Unlike SSRIs)

No "brain zaps," no severe dizziness, no electric shock sensations, no intense flu-like syndrome. Wellbutrin is generally considered one of the easier antidepressants to taper off.

🚨 Contact Your Prescriber If:

  • Symptoms are severe or getting worse after week 3
  • Seizure activity (rare but important — Wellbutrin lowers seizure threshold)
  • Suicidal thoughts or severe hopelessness
  • You feel like your original depression symptoms are fully returning

🔍 Withdrawal vs. Relapse — How to Tell

▼

This is the most important thing to track. The key difference is timing and trajectory.

Discontinuation (Normal)

  • Appears within days of reducing dose
  • Peaks at 1–2 weeks
  • Improves week by week
  • Resolves within 2–4 weeks
  • Feels like a physical adjustment

Relapse (Worth Checking)

  • Emerges gradually, often 4–8 weeks out
  • Gets progressively worse over time
  • Feels like original symptoms returning
  • Doesn't resolve on its own
  • Affects function at work, relationships

💡 Simple Rule of Thumb

If you feel better each week → it's withdrawal. If you feel worse three weeks or more after stopping → check in with your prescriber. Use your symptom log in this app to track the trend!

✅ How to Know You're Ready to Stop

▼

Green Lights — Good Signs to Proceed

  • ✅ You've been stable at your current dose for 4+ weeks with manageable symptoms
  • ✅ No major life stressors expected in the next 2–3 months
  • ✅ You have coping tools in place (exercise, sleep hygiene, stress management, therapy if applicable)
  • ✅ Your TRT levels are optimized — testosterone plays a big role in mood and motivation
  • ✅ You feel emotionally equipped to handle a temporary dip
  • ✅ You and your prescriber are aligned on the timeline

Red Lights — Consider Waiting

  • ❌ Current mood or depression symptoms are still significant
  • ❌ High stress period (job change, relationship stress, family crisis)
  • ❌ Symptoms worsening week to week at the current dose
  • ❌ TRT or other hormone levels are not yet optimized
  • ❌ Suicidal ideation or severe hopelessness present

💉 TRT Guide for Women

▼

📅 What to Expect: Your TRT Timeline

Based on your TRT start date. Testosterone therapy works gradually - here's what happens week by week.

Weeks 1-2: Building Phase
  • Testosterone levels beginning to rise
  • May notice subtle mood lift or increased sense of well-being
  • Some women feel slightly more energetic
  • Possible mild oily skin or small breakouts (temporary)
  • Sleep may start to deepen
Weeks 3-4: Early Benefits
  • Mental clarity improving - less brain fog
  • Energy more consistent throughout the day
  • Motivation starting to return
  • May notice increased confidence
  • First hints of libido returning
  • Workouts feel slightly easier
Weeks 5-8: Noticeable Changes
  • Libido significantly improved
  • Better orgasm quality and sensitivity
  • Mood more stable and positive
  • Increased muscle tone with exercise
  • Fat loss easier, especially around midsection
  • Sleep quality improved
  • Anxiety often reduced
Weeks 8-12: Optimization
  • Full effects realized - this is your "new normal"
  • Steady state testosterone levels achieved
  • Time to check labs and adjust dose if needed
  • Sexual function optimized
  • Body composition changes visible
  • Confidence and assertiveness improved
Month 3-6+: Long-term Benefits
  • Bone density improvements (not visible but important)
  • Continued muscle/strength gains
  • Sustained libido and sexual satisfaction
  • Overall quality of life significantly better
  • Many women report feeling "like themselves again"

  • Overall stamina improved
Sexual Health
  • Sexual thoughts return

Physical Changes

  • Skin may look healthier
  • Stronger during workouts

⚠️ What to Watch For (Side Effects)

Most side effects indicate your dose may need adjustment. Report these to your provider.

Common & Usually Temporary
  • Oily skin or mild acne (weeks 1-4)
  • Slight hair texture changes
  • Mild water retention initially
  • Increased body heat
  • Mood swings during adjustment
Signs Dose May Be Too High
  • Persistent acne (especially cystic)
  • Unusual hair growth (face/body)
  • Hair thinning on head
  • Voice deepening
  • Clitoral sensitivity changes
  • Increased aggression or irritability
  • Disrupted menstrual cycle

Action: Contact your provider - dose likely needs lowering

❌ Signs TRT May Not Be Working

If you've been on TRT for 8-12 weeks and experience these, talk to your provider:

  • No improvement in energy or fatigue
  • Libido still absent or very low
  • No change in mood or brain fog
  • Labs show testosterone isn't rising appropriately
  • Estrogen too high (testosterone converting)

Possible causes: Dose too low, absorption issues, high SHBG binding the testosterone, or testosterone converting to estrogen. Labs will help identify the issue.

⚡ NAD+ Therapy Guide

▼

🔬 What is NAD+ and Why Does It Matter?

NAD+ (Nicotinamide Adenine Dinucleotide) is a coenzyme found in every cell of your body. It's essential for:

  • Energy production - Converts food into cellular energy (ATP)
  • DNA repair - Helps maintain healthy cells and slow aging
  • Brain function - Supports neurotransmitter production (dopamine, serotonin)
  • Metabolism - Regulates circadian rhythm and metabolic processes
  • Cellular health - Activates sirtuins (longevity proteins)

The problem: NAD+ levels naturally decline with age - by age 50, you may have only 50% of what you had at 20. Supplementing helps restore optimal levels.

First Injection
  • May feel a "flush" or warmth (normal, temporary)
  • Some experience mild nausea (usually with IV, less with injections)
  • Possible boost in mental clarity same day
  • Better sleep that night for many women
Weeks 1-2
  • Energy levels beginning to stabilize
  • Mental fog starting to clear
  • May notice improved focus
  • Sleep quality often improves
  • Subtle mood improvements
Weeks 3-4
  • More consistent energy throughout the day
  • Improved exercise recovery
  • Better mental stamina
  • Cellular repair processes accelerating
  • Some notice improved skin quality
Month 2+
  • Full benefits realized
  • Sustained energy and mental clarity
  • Enhanced TRT benefits (they work synergistically)
  • Better overall cellular function
  • Many report feeling "younger" or more vital

Normal reactions (usually temporary):

Tip: Stay well-hydrated before and after NAD+ therapy. This minimizes side effects significantly.

🌿 Lifestyle Optimization for Hormone Therapy

▼

😴 Sleep Optimization

  • Aim for 7-9 hours per night
  • Keep consistent sleep/wake times
  • Cool, dark room (65-68°F)
  • No screens 1 hour before bed
  • Magnesium glycinate before bed (400mg)
  • Avoid alcohol - it disrupts hormones

Why it matters: Sleep is when your body produces hormones and repairs cells. Poor sleep = poor hormone function.

💪 Exercise

  • Strength training: 2-4x/week - amplifies TRT benefits
  • Walking: 30+ min daily - supports metabolism
  • HIIT: 1-2x/week max - avoid overtraining
  • Don't exercise too close to bedtime
  • Listen to your body - rest when needed

Why it matters: Resistance training increases testosterone sensitivity and helps build lean muscle.

🥗 Nutrition

  • Protein: 0.8-1g per lb body weight
  • Healthy fats: Avocado, olive oil, nuts (hormone building blocks)
  • Limit sugar: Spikes insulin, disrupts hormones
  • Zinc-rich foods: Oysters, beef, pumpkin seeds
  • Cruciferous veggies: Help metabolize estrogen
  • Stay hydrated - especially with NAD+

🧘 Stress Management

  • Chronic stress = high cortisol = low testosterone
  • Daily relaxation practice (even 10 min)
  • Morning sunlight exposure (helps cortisol rhythm)
  • Limit caffeine after noon
  • Breathwork, meditation, or gentle yoga

Key insight: Cortisol and testosterone compete. Reduce stress to maximize TRT benefits.

🚨 When to Call Your Doctor

▼

🆘 Call Immediately

  • Chest pain or pressure
  • Severe headache (worst of your life)
  • Sudden vision changes
  • Severe allergic reaction (swelling, difficulty breathing)
  • Signs of blood clot (leg swelling, pain, warmth)
  • Thoughts of self-harm

📞 Contact Within 24-48 Hours

  • Persistent severe acne
  • Significant hair loss
  • Voice changes (deepening)
  • Unusual vaginal bleeding
  • Severe mood changes (rage, depression)
  • Swelling in hands/feet that doesn't resolve
  • Signs of infection at injection site

📋 Discuss at Next Visit

  • Mild acne that's manageable
  • Slight increase in body hair
  • Oily skin
  • Energy not improving after 8 weeks
  • Libido not improving after 12 weeks
  • Questions about adjusting dose

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

▼
Q: How long until I feel TRT working?

A: Most women notice subtle improvements in energy and mood within 2-4 weeks. Libido improvements typically take 6-12 weeks. Full benefits are usually realized by 3 months.

Q: Will TRT make me look masculine?

A: At proper female doses, no. Masculinizing effects only occur with doses much higher than therapeutic levels. If you notice voice changes or unusual hair growth, your dose may need adjustment.

Q: Can I stop TRT once I start?

A: Yes. Unlike men, women's natural testosterone production isn't typically suppressed by TRT at therapeutic doses. If you stop, you'll gradually return to your pre-treatment baseline over a few weeks.

Q: Does the day of the week matter for my TRT shot?

A: Consistency matters more than the specific day. Pick a day that works for your schedule and stick to it. This maintains stable hormone levels.

Q: How often should I do NAD+?

A: Most protocols suggest weekly or bi-weekly for the first month (loading phase), then weekly or every other week for maintenance. Your provider will customize based on your response.

Q: Can I exercise on shot days?

A: Yes! Some women prefer to work out on shot days when they feel a slight boost. Just avoid intense exercise immediately after the injection to prevent irritation at the site.

Q: What if I miss a dose?

A: Take it as soon as you remember if it's within 1-2 days. If it's close to your next scheduled dose, skip the missed one and continue your regular schedule. Don't double up.

Q: Will TRT affect my period?

A: It can. Some women experience lighter or irregular periods. If you're perimenopausal or post-menopausal, this is less relevant. Discuss any significant changes with your provider.
